Services

You can choose from the following options:

Au Pair ( 25-30 hours / week)

  • works 5h / day, 5 days / week
  • Baby sits 2-3 evenings / week
  • receives 2 free days + 3 evenings off
  • advised pay from £75 
  • no sole care for children under 2

Au Pair Plus ( 30-35 hours / week)

  • works 6-7h/ day, 5days / week
  • Baby sits 2-3 evenings / week
  • receives 2 days and 3 evenings off
  • advised pay from £90 
  • no sole care for children under 2

Mother`s Help ( 35+ hours / week)

These are candidates who are willing to engage 

in higher levels of responsibility, 

without holding specific qualifications for this sector.

  • works 5/6 days / week , up to 10h / day
  • Baby sits 2-3 evenings / week
  • receives minimum 1 day and 3 evenings off
  • advised pay from £140
  • longer hours and younger children

Mother’s help – A mother’s help is a carer who has got a genuine interest in children. The mother’s help will have at least 1 year’s relevant experience and / or a qualification. Duties regarding children will be according to her experience, the type of household and the ages of the children. Depending on the childcare responsibilities expected, she will be able to help around the house with light housework and run errands. If she is experienced and confident enough, she can take sole charge of the children at times. Unlike most nannies, mothers’ helps will normally do some light housework.



With all of the options, this is only the recommended pay, most of the families offer more pocket money.

Some families might offer extra benefits, such as telephone, car, laptop, etc). The extra benefits will be at the discretion of the Family.
