The Au Pair

Cultural Exchange Programme

Families

About: This excellent opportunity has arisen under the agreement of the member States of the Council of Europe, “considering that the aim of the Council of Europe is to achieve greater unity between its members, in particular for the purpose of facilitating their social progress; noting that in Europe more and more younger persons, especially girls, are going abroad to be placed Au Pair”.

Therefore, the above mentioned placement is the temporary reception by families, in exchange for certain services, of young foreigners who come to improve their linguistic and possibly professional knowledge, as well as their general culture by building up a better knowledge of the country where they are hosted.

 

You get the opportunity to have a helping hand in your house and childcare for your children, whilst you are working.


Requirements: as a receiving family you need to:

  • Be able to offer full board/lodging and a separate room.
  • Be able to give adequate time to attend language courses as well as for cultural and professional improvement.
  • Making sure that the Au Pair has at least one full free day/ week, not less than one such free day in every month being a Sunday, and shall have full opportunity to take part in religious worship.
  • Making sure that the Au Pair will receive a certain sum of money (known as pocket money); the amount of which will be negotiated by our company, on the behalf of the Au Pair.
  • You will be protected by a written agreement. At your discretion, you may include extra benefits that you might be able to offer to your Au Pair).
  • Treat the Au Pair as a family member.

 

Benefits: The Au Pair:

  • Shall not be less than 18 or more than 30 years of age (exceptions to the upper age limit may be granted by the competent authority of the receiving country, when justified).
  • Will have a medical certificate, declaring the general state of health.
  • The rights and obligations of the Au Pair and the Family shall be subject of an agreement in writing (the agreement will specify the manner in which the Au Pair is to share the life of the receiving family, while at the same time enjoying a specific degree of independence).
  • The Au Pair will join the family’s services (consisting in participation in a day-to-day family duty).
  • Will have a DBS check (previously known as CRB).
  • Will have adequate references.
  • Is caring and kind.
  • Will have a certain level of English/ other languages
  • Is an EU member – meets all immigration and other legislation requirements.

 

Our company will carefully screen and pre-interview them, on your behalf. After matching you with the potential Au Pair, we encourage you to interview (on the phone, Skype) at least two times before you can agree. Therefore, you will take part in the selection. Once both happy and compatible, we will do the rest!
